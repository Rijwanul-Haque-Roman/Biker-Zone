Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if it's more than 5-7 years old, as materials degrade over time. Check for visible cracks, dents, or damage on the outer shell. Inspect the inner foam for signs of degradation, such as crumbling or brittleness. Ensure straps are in good condition and not frayed. If the helmet has been in a crash or no longer fits snugly, replace it immediately for optimal safety.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding your bike after storage, check the tires for proper inflation and any signs of damage. Inspect the brakes to ensure they are responsive, and test the lights and signals for functionality. Lubricate the chain and moving parts, and verify that the fuel and oil levels are appropriate. Take a short test ride in a controlled environment to ensure everything is functioning properly before embarking on longer journeys.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.
